The Jagannath Temple is an important Hindu temple dedicated to Jagannath, a form of Vishnu, one of the trinity of supreme divinity in Hinduism. Puri is in the state of Odisha, on the eastern coast of India. King Indradyumna of Avanti has built the main temple of Jagannath at Puri.

The Puri temple is famous for its annual Ratha Yatra, or chariot festival, in which the three principal deities are pulled on huge and elaborately decorated temple cars, Worship is performed by the Bhil Sawar tribal priests as well as priests of other communities in the Jagannath temple.Read more




Rules & Guidelines


Puri, a captivating city on India's eastern coast, offers a mix of cultural richness and natural beauty. While exploring, keep in mind that the Jagannath Temple, a spiritual epicenter, has dress code restrictions, requiring modest attire. As you wander through the vibrant markets, relish local seafood delicacies like the famous Puri Mahaprasad.


Puri's pristine beaches provide a serene escape, but note that swimming can have restrictions due to strong currents. Visit the Sun Temple in Konark, a UNESCO World Heritage site, showcasing architectural brilliance. The Chandrabhaga Beach is another gem for tranquil moments.


When it comes to attire, lightweight, comfortable clothing is advisable, especially if you plan to visit during the hot and humid months. Don't forget sunscreen and a hat for sun protection.


Puri also hosts the grand Rath Yatra, a chariot festival attracting millions. During this time, the city buzzes with energy, but be prepared for increased crowds and potential travel disruptions.


Respect local customs, such as taking off shoes before entering certain places. Embrace the vibrant culture, savor local cuisine, and adhere to any religious or safety guidelines for a fulfilling experience in this culturally rich and enchanting destination.
